2 Equation in Multiple Equation Format

\[\begin{aligned} y_{ij} = \beta_{0j} + \beta_{1j} x + e_{ij} \\ \beta_{0j} = \gamma_{00} + \gamma_{01} \text{sector} + u_{0j} \\ \beta_{1j} = \gamma_{10} + \gamma_{11} \text{sector} + u_{0j} \end{aligned} \tag{1}\]

Random Effects Are Optional

The random effects in Equation 1 are optional in the sense that they may or may not be included. Often only a random intercept is included. Inclusion of multiple random slopes may lead to non-convergence of the model.

3 Intra-Class Correlation Coefficient

\[\text{icc} = \frac{\text{var}(u_{0j})}{\text{var}(u_{0j}) + \text{var}(e_{ij})}\]

4 How Many Levels?

For the \(i^{th}\) student in the \(j^{th}\) school in the \(k^{th}\) district.

\[\begin{aligned} y_{ijk} = \pi_{0jk} + \pi_{1jk} a + e_{ijk} \\ \color{red}{\text{there are p level 1 coefficients}} \\ \pi_{pjk} = \beta_{0pk} + \beta_{1pk} + r_{pjk} \\ \color{red}{\text{group specific effect for the jth group}} \\ \color{red}{\text{there are q level 2 coefficients}} \\ \beta_{pqk} = \gamma_{0pq} + \gamma_{1pq} w + u_{pqk} \\ \color{red}{\text{there are p*q level 3 coefficients}} \\ \end{aligned} \tag{2}\]

5 A Single Level Equation

Using substitution, we re-write Equation 1.

\[y_{ij} = \gamma_{00} + \gamma_{01} \text{sector} + \gamma_{10} x + \gamma_{11} \text{sector} \times x + e_{ij} + u_{0j} + u_{1j} \times x \tag{3}\]

Let’s just call everything a \(\beta\).

\[y_{ij} = \beta_{0} + \beta_{1} \text{sector} + \beta_{2} x + \beta_{3} \text{sector} \times x + e_{ij} + u_{0j} + u_{1j} \times x \tag{4}\]

\(x\) is \(\text{ses}\)
